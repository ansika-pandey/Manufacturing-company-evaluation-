# Manufacturing Company Evaluation

## Overview
This project evaluates Indian manufacturing companies using a structured business evaluation framework. Each company is assessed based on eligibility, technical differentiation, leadership quality, market tailwinds, growth signals, systems maturity, and leadership succession.

## Objective
- Research manufacturing companies using public sources.
- Apply a consistent scoring framework.
- Identify high-potential companies based on operational and business criteria.

## Evaluation Framework
### Eligibility Gates
- E1: Producer
- E2: Accessible

### Scoring Criteria
- C3: Differentiation
- C4: Decision-Maker Quality
- C5: Growing Sector
- C6: Growth Signals
- C7: Systems Maturity
- C8: Leadership Succession

## Disclaimer
This project was created for learning and portfolio purposes. The evaluations are based on publicly available information and may change as companies update their business or financial information.
## Methodology

The companies were evaluated using a structured research process:

1. Selected Pune as the target city.
2. Shortlisted manufacturing companies from official websites, Google, LinkedIn, IndiaMART, TradeIndia, and business directories.
3. Verified eligibility using:
   - E1: Producer
   - E2: Operational presence in India
4. Evaluated eligible companies on:
   - C3: Differentiation
   - C4: Decision-Maker Quality
   - C5: Growing Sector
   - C6: Growth Signals
   - C7: Systems Maturity
   - C8: Leadership Succession
5. Assigned a Federer Score (0–100) and categorized each company.
6. Documented evidence and sources for every evaluation.
